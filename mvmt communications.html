Karthik Ganapathy

Karthik Ganapathy is an experienced communicator with deep roots in the progressive movement. Karthik served as the Battleground States Communications Director for Bernie Sanders’ 2016 presidential campaign, after leading the campaign’s communications team in New Hampshire.

Since then, he helped the ACLU launch People Power, the organization’s first-ever attempt at organizing volunteers, ran the communications office of then-DNC Deputy Chair Keith Ellison, and played a senior role in helping MoveOn communicate about its midterm election work in 2018. Karthik began his career as an aide to then-Public Advocate Bill de Blasio, and feels at home in the movement for climate justice.

Mike Casca

Mike Casca served as Rapid Response Director to Bernie Sanders’ 2016 presidential campaign. He went on to be Deputy Communications Director in Sanders’ Senate office following the historic primary. He moved to New York City in 2017 to work as Communications Director for Mayor Bill de Blasio, who that fall became the first Democrat re-elected mayor in the city in more than 30 years.

He began his political career in Rep. Keith Ellison’s office, starting as a press intern and eventually becoming Communications Director, where he helped lead strategy to raise wages for federal contract workers, stop the Trans-Pacific Partnership and protect Social Security, Medicare and Medicaid.
