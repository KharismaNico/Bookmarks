TAMPA -- A man wont be prosecuted for beating the pedophile who raped and choked him as a child when the two were mistakenly put in the same jail cell.

Jason Flores, who was arrested for a string of crimes in October, still will have to serve more than two years in state prison for other felonies. The Hillsborough County State Attorneys Office said Monday it dropped a battery charge that could have made his prison sentence even longer.

By a fluke, Flores found himself at the Orient Road Jail face to face with Kevin Kinder, the man who had raped and choked him years earlier. Flores attacked him, authorities said.

In December, investigators said, Flores attacked another inmate, a man accused of raping a 9-year-old girl, in the recreation yard at the same jail.

Flores, 23, had pleaded guilty to charges of car theft, resisting arrest without violence and burglary, as well as to violating his probation on charges of aggravated assault on an officer, burglary, possessing burglary tools and car theft.

Rick Escobar, Flores lawyer, said the charges could have brought him 20 years in prison. Instead, Hillsborough Circuit Judge Anthony Black agreed to the states request that Flores not receive such a long sentence.

Flores term will be followed by two years probation. He also will receive psychiatric treatment.

Three mental health experts testified on Flores behalf at the sentencing, saying the psychological counseling he had received in the aftermath of being raped was inadequate.

An expert from the University of South Florida said Flores suffered from severe post-traumatic stress disorder and that his accidental encounter with Kinder triggered a long-stifled healing process.

At age 10, Jason was brutally raped, strangled and left for dead in a field, Escobar said. You cant have greater trauma, in my opinion. He had been frozen in time all these years.

Kinder pleaded guilty in 1992 to performing lewd acts on four boys ages 11-14, including Flores.
